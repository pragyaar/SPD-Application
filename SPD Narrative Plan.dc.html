Four opportunities, in priority order

i.Turn the Summer 2026 outdoor tour into a content engine+
The tour runs July to early September, exactly when this role begins. Every venue is a story: new city, new audience, new backdrop. A tour-diary format (60 to 90 second recap reels, dancer takeovers, audience reaction capture) compounds across the run and gives every venue partner assets to amplify. This is the single highest-leverage window of the year, and it should not pass undocumented.
ii.Launch TikTok with Bharatanatyam-native short-form+
Adavus, rhythm cycles, eye and hand vocabulary, the 40-minute make-up ritual: Bharatanatyam is structurally suited to short-form in a way most contemporary dance is not. A disciplined three-format pilot (explainer / process / performance excerpt) reaches the younger and newer audiences the job pack names, without diluting brand integrity.
iii.Wake the archive+
Years of professional footage and photography already exist. Repurposed systematically (one shoot, ten assets), the archive can sustain a rolling editorial calendar at near-zero marginal cost, exactly as the job pack asks: “edit and repurpose archive material into fresh branded assets.”
iv.Build the climate & community thread into the narrative framework+
The job pack's most distinctive duty: embedding environmental justice and decolonising approaches to climate in SPD's narrative. The work already carries it: The Rite of Spring is Mother Earth's journey from birth to destruction to deliverance, performed outdoors in shared public space. That is not a bolt-on sustainability post; it is a narrative thread connecting repertoire, communities and place.

The first 90 days, timed to your tour.

Whoever takes this role walks in mid-tour. That is not a complication; it is the best onboarding a storyteller could ask for. This plan starts where SPD will actually be in August 2026: on the road, outdoors, in front of new audiences.

Actions

  • +Join the tour on the road: capture stills, reels and dancer voices at every reachable date
  • +Audit brand voice with Seeta and the team: what SPD sounds like, and what it never sounds like
  • +Baseline all analytics; set up unified scheduling and reporting (Hootsuite-certified workflow)
  • +Quick accessibility pass: captions, subtitles and alt text on all active content
  • +Map the archive: every asset, every right, every permission, GDPR-clean from day one
Why this first? Trust before voice. The job pack calls this role the guardian of SPD's voice and aesthetic, and guardianship is earned by listening. Meanwhile the tour is generating once-a-year content that must be captured now, even if much of it is published later.

Actions

  • +Build the narrative framework around your four value areas, taken straight from the job pack: Artistic · Talent Development · Community · Organisational
  • +Launch the TikTok pilot with three repeatable formats tested against real data
  • +Begin the archive remix series across Instagram and YouTube Shorts
  • +Stand up the rolling 90-day editorial calendar: proactive, not reactive, as the pack demands
  • +Open reciprocal amplification conversations with South Asian artists and dance influencers
Why now? Tour momentum becomes structure. Your job pack asks for someone to translate SPD's artistic, talent development, community and organisational value into clear, confident narratives. September turns a month of captured material into exactly that: one durable framework, four value areas, every post tracing back to one of them.

Actions

  • +First quarterly impact report: growth, engagement and audience insight mapped to organisational objectives
  • +Build the ACE Annual Survey-ready measurement framework: funder reporting without the scramble
  • +Launch LinkedIn presence centred on Talent Development Programme stories
  • +Plan the autumn/winter narrative arc with the artistic and producing team
  • +Review, kill or scale every format based on 60 days of evidence
Why this matters? Success here is more than metrics, the pack says so itself. But funders need numbers, and the Executive Director should never have to chase them. By day 90: a measurable growth story, a working editorial system, and a team that trusts the person telling its story.
